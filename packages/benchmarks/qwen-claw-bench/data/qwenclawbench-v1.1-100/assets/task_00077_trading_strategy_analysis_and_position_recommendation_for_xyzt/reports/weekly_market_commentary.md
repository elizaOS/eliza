# Weekly Market Commentary
## Week of November 11–15, 2024
## Prepared by: Internal Research Team
## Distribution: Trading Desk, Portfolio Management

---

### Market Overview

Equity markets demonstrated broad resilience this week despite mixed economic data. The S&P 500 gained 0.4% on the week, while the Nasdaq Composite edged up 0.2%. Small caps underperformed with the Russell 2000 declining 0.6%.

### Key Themes

#### 1. Fed Pivot Speculation Continues

Market participants continue to price in a potential Fed pivot toward rate cuts in Q1 2025. The November FOMC statement was largely in line with expectations, but the upcoming release of meeting minutes (November 20) could provide additional color on the committee's deliberations. We expect the minutes to reveal a more divided committee than the statement suggested.

#### 2. Technology Sector Headwinds

The technology sector may face headwinds from rising yields in the near term. The 10-year Treasury yield climbed 8 basis points this week to 4.52%, driven by stronger-than-expected retail sales data. Higher yields compress growth stock valuations and could weigh on tech multiples if the trend continues.

That said, the fundamental earnings picture for large-cap tech remains solid, and any pullback should be viewed in the context of the sector's strong year-to-date performance.

#### 3. Healthcare Sector Momentum

Healthcare continues to show relative strength, supported by favorable demographic trends and a robust pipeline of FDA approvals. We remain constructive on the sector and see potential for continued outperformance through year-end.

#### 4. Earnings Season Winding Down

With approximately 90% of S&P 500 companies having reported Q3 earnings, the blended earnings growth rate stands at 5.8% YoY — ahead of the 4.2% consensus estimate at the start of the season. Revenue growth has been more modest at 2.1%.

### Volatility Outlook

The VIX settled at 22.5, above its long-term average of ~19 but well below stress levels. We characterize the current regime as "elevated but manageable." Options markets are pricing in slightly higher implied volatility for the next 30 days, likely reflecting uncertainty around the upcoming CPI release and Fed minutes.

### Sector Recommendations

| Sector | View | Rationale |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Technology | Neutral | Yield headwinds offset by strong fundamentals |
| Healthcare | Overweight | Relative strength, favorable catalysts |
| Energy | Underweight | Inventory builds, demand uncertainty |
| Financials | Neutral | Benefiting from yield curve normalization |

### Looking Ahead

Key events next week include:
- FOMC Meeting Minutes (Wednesday, Nov 20)
- Initial Jobless Claims (Thursday, Nov 21)
- CPI Release (Friday, Nov 22)

We advise maintaining current positioning and avoiding large directional bets ahead of the CPI release, which has the potential to significantly move markets in either direction.
