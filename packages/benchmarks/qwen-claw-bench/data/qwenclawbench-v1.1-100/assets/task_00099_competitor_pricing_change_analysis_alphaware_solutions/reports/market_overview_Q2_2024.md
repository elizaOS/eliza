# Q2 2024 Mid-Market SaaS — Quarterly Market Overview

**Prepared by:** Market Intelligence Team  
**Date:** June 10, 2024  
**Classification:** Internal — Confidential  

---

## Executive Summary

Q2 2024 has been a transformative quarter for the mid-market SaaS project management and collaboration space. The dominant theme has been the rapid acceleration of AI feature adoption, with three out of five top competitors launching AI-powered capabilities during this period. Simultaneously, we are observing sustained downward pricing pressure on enterprise-tier plans, with average prices declining 10–15% quarter-over-quarter.

Customer acquisition costs (CAC) continue to rise across the sector, up approximately 18% YoY, driven by increased competition for the same buyer personas and saturation of traditional digital marketing channels. In response, several competitors have begun bundling AI features into existing plans — or offering them as low-cost add-ons — as a retention and upsell strategy.

---

## Key Trends

### 1. AI Feature Adoption Accelerates

The AI arms race in mid-market SaaS has intensified significantly in Q2:

- **BetaFlow** launched an AI-powered project forecasting tool in April 2024
- **Nimbus Workspace** introduced AI writing and summarization features in May 2024
- **TaskForge Pro** rolled out AI-based resource optimization in early June 2024
- **AlphaWare Solutions** and **CollabSync** have not yet announced AI features as of this report's publication date (June 10, 2024)

Industry analysts at Forrester and Gartner have both flagged AI capability as a "table stakes" requirement for mid-market SaaS vendors by end of 2024. Vendors without a credible AI story risk losing enterprise deals to competitors who can demonstrate productivity gains.

### 2. Enterprise Pricing Under Pressure

Average enterprise plan pricing across the top 10 mid-market SaaS vendors has declined by approximately **12%** in Q2 2024 compared to Q1:

| Metric | Q1 2024 | Q2 2024 | Change |
|--------|---------|---------|--------|
| Avg. Enterprise Plan Price | $305/mo | $268/mo | -12.1% |
| Avg. Included Seats | 55 | 72 | +30.9% |
| Vendors Offering AI | 2 of 10 | 5 of 10 | +150% |

This pricing compression is being driven by:
- New entrants offering aggressive introductory pricing
- Established vendors cutting prices to defend market share
- Buyers demanding more value (seats, features) per dollar
- AI features being used as justification for maintaining or increasing prices

### 3. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) Rising

CAC for mid-market SaaS enterprise deals has increased to an estimated **$12,400** per new logo, up from **$10,500** in Q2 2023. Key drivers include:

- Google Ads CPC for SaaS keywords up 22% YoY
- LinkedIn advertising costs up 15% YoY
- Longer sales cycles (avg. 68 days vs. 54 days a year ago)
- More stakeholders involved in buying decisions (avg. 4.2 vs. 3.1)

As a result, **customer retention has become even more critical**. Competitors are increasingly bundling AI features and expanding seat counts to reduce churn and increase lifetime value.

### 4. Bundling AI as a Retention Strategy

A notable trend in Q2 is the strategic bundling of AI capabilities:

- **BetaFlow** includes AI in all plans above their starter tier at no extra cost
- **Nimbus Workspace** offers AI as a $29/mo add-on per workspace
- **TaskForge Pro** bundles AI into their enterprise plan and raised the price by $20/mo

This bundling approach serves dual purposes: it differentiates the offering in competitive deals and it creates switching costs that improve retention. Vendors who cannot offer AI features — even basic ones — are increasingly at a disadvantage in enterprise procurement evaluations.

## Implications for Our Strategy

1. **Our AI advantage is narrowing.** With 3 of 5 top competitors now offering AI, our "AI-included" Enterprise plan is less differentiated than it was in Q1.

2. **Our pricing remains competitive.** At $279/mo for 75 seats with AI included, we offer strong value. However, the market is moving toward lower price points with more seats.

3. **Watch AlphaWare closely.** They raised prices in March despite market trends, which is unusual. They may be preparing a repositioning move — potentially bundling new features to justify the higher price, or they may be forced to cut prices if they lose deals.

4. **Consider proactive pricing review.** Given the 10-15% average price decline in the market, we should evaluate whether a modest price reduction or seat increase would strengthen our competitive position.
